D — Grammar Challenge
Choose the correct option. Circle A, B or C.
Reported speech: tenses shift back — will → would · is → was · can → could · have to → had to
Inversion: after negative adverbials — Hardly had he sent (not "Hardly he had sent")
It's time + past simple: present meaning — It's time companies stopped pretending
Question 1 — Reported Speech
"The CEO said the return to office ___ from Monday 7 July."
A   will take effect B   would take effect C   takes effect
Question 2 — Inversion
"Hardly ___ the memo when his phone started buzzing with Slack notifications."
A   he had sent B   did he send C   had he sent
Question 3 — Passive Infinitive
"The employees demanded ___ before the policy was announced."
A   to be consulted B   to consult C   being consulting
Question 4 — It's time + past simple
"It's time companies ___ that flexibility is an expectation, not a perk."
A   stop pretending B   to stop pretending C   stopped pretending
Question 5 — Mixed Conditional
"If the company ___ a hybrid model, employees ___ for new jobs by now."
A   hadn't scrapped / wouldn't be looking B   didn't scrap / wouldn't look C   hadn't scrapped / wouldn't have looked
