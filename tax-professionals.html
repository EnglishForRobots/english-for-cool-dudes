2. Reading & Comprehension

Read the short case study below, then answer the questions that follow.

Case Study: Alexâ€™s Annual Tax Filing

Alex, a freelance graphic designer, is preparing to file his annual **tax return**. He earned a total of $75,000 last year from various clients. He knows he can claim several **deductions**, including $2,000 for office supplies and $3,500 for his work-related travel expenses. Additionally, he had a significant **capital gain** of $10,000 from the sale of some stocks he had owned for several years.

Alex is trying to determine his final **taxable income**. He hopes that by using the available deductions, he can lower his overall **income tax** burden. He is also reviewing his **filing status** to make sure he is using the most beneficial one, and is considering if he qualifies for any additional **exemptions** that might further reduce his liability.

1. What is the primary purpose of Alex's tax return?

  • To apply for a new loan.
  • To report his income and calculate his tax liability.
  • To get a tax refund.

2. What is Alex considering to lower his taxable income?

  • Claiming deductions and exemptions.
  • Selling more stocks for a capital gain.
  • Increasing his travel expenses.

3. The $10,000 from Alex's stock sale is an example of what?

  • Gross income.
  • Deductions.
  • Capital gains.
