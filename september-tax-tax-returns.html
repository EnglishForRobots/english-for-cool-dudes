Exercise 2: Case Study: The Canary Wharf Contractor

Client Scenario: Mr. Davies

Mr. Davies, a freelance IT contractor based near Canary Wharf, is submitting his **Self Assessment Tax Return**. He is required to do this because his income from his contract work is not taxed at source via PAYE. He has received his **P60** from his only employed role earlier in the year, which summarises his previous **Income from Employment**.

You advise him on maximizing his **Allowable Expenses**. He can deduct costs for his new work laptop, his professional indemnity insurance, and the business mileage he claimed while visiting clients. Crucially, his **Personal Allowance** (the tax-free portion of income) will apply, though you note that for very high earners in London, this benefit can be **tapered** (reduced).

Once submitted, **HMRC** will send the official **Notice of Assessment**. If this shows he owes more tax, it's called a **Tax Due** or **Additional Payment**. If he believes the assessment is wrong, he must lodge an **appeal** within the required timeframe, usually 30 days.
