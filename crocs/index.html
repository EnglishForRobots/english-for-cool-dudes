Crocs' China Strategy: A Marketing Masterclass

This lesson explores how the Crocs brand has successfully adapted its marketing strategy to appeal to China's Gen Z.

Short Reading Comprehension

Read the text below about Crocs' success and answer the questions that follow. **Click on the bold words to see their definitions**.

While brands like Nike are struggling in China, a less likely American brand is thriving: Crocs. Their recent growth in the Chinese market has been remarkable, while their North American revenue has declined. The key to their success is their ability to understand China's Gen Z, one of the most lucrative consumer segments. They've adapted their global slogan and tapped into the younger generation's desire for self-expression. By featuring local celebrity endorsers and working with Chinese designers like Feng Chen Wang, Crocs has also managed to downplay its American roots, a clever move given the current geopolitical climate. This ability to adapt to local culture and tastes has been a game changer for the brand, proving that listening to consumers is more important than having a loud, Western voice.

Grammar Focus: Word Formation

**Instructions:** Change the word in brackets where necessary to complete the sentences.

üìö Quick Explanation:

In English, we often change the form of a word (e.g., from a verb to a noun or vice-versa) to fit a sentence. For example, the verb to **grow** becomes the noun **growth**, or the verb to **succeed** becomes the noun **success**. In business, this is very common!

1. The new campaign aims to increase consumer (aware).

2. The company's recent (collaborate) with a local designer was very successful.

3. This new product is a (signify) factor in the company's turnaround.

4. Crocs' strategy in China is a perfect (demonstrate) of smart marketing.

5. The brand wants to (influence) young consumers to express themselves.
