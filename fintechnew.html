❓ Exercise 2: Case Study: Launching a Digital Product

Scenario: Monolith Money's Expansion

Monolith Money, a leading London **Neobank**, is preparing to launch across Europe. A major concern is **scalability**; their back-end infrastructure must be able to handle millions of new users without crashing. Before onboarding any customer, they must ensure rigorous **KYC** procedures are followed, including biometric scans and passport verification, to comply with strict **AML** (Anti-Money Laundering) regulations.

The bank is also integrating a third-party budgeting app. This integration is handled entirely through a secure **API** endpoint, allowing the third party read-only access to customer transaction data after explicit consent is given. Their tech lead also noted that while their core banking system is centralised, they are exploring the use of **Blockchain** technology for their cross-border payment rails, leveraging its **decentralisation** for faster, cheaper transfers.
