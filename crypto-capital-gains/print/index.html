B — Match the Terms
Draw a line connecting each term to its correct definition.
💡 Did you know? Many clients assume "I never cashed out" means "I don't owe tax." In most countries, a crypto-to-crypto swap is treated as a disposal — triggering a taxable capital gain even if no euros ever touched a bank account. This is one of the most expensive misunderstandings in crypto tax.

Definitions
ASelling a losing asset deliberately to offset taxable gains
BThe original purchase price of an asset
CProfit from selling an asset above what it cost
DAccounting method: the first units bought are treated as the first units sold
EOECD framework requiring exchanges to report user transactions automatically
FA taxable profit that has actually been made — the asset has been sold or swapped

C — True or False?
Circle T (True) or F (False). If false, correct the mistake below.
StatementT  /  F
1. Swapping Bitcoin for Ethereum is NOT a taxable event — because no cash was involved.
2. An "unrealised gain" is taxable only when the asset has been sold or swapped.
3. CARF is an OECD framework requiring crypto exchanges to report user data to tax authorities.
4. Tax-loss harvesting is illegal when applied to cryptocurrency.
5. In many countries, a longer holding period can lead to a lower tax rate on capital gains.
6. "FIFO" stands for "First In, First Out" — a method for calculating which units were sold.
