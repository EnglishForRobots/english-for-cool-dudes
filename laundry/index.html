1. Why were the cash deposits flagged by the system?

  • They were made in a foreign currency.
  • They were just under the reporting threshold (Smurfing).
  • The client forgot to sign the deposit slip.

2. What is the main problem with "Oceanic Trade Ltd"?

  • The bank doesn't know who the real human owner (Beneficial Owner) is.
  • They are trading in illegal goods.
  • They are not paying enough fees.

3. Why must the account manager remain silent?

  • Because it is rude to discuss money.
  • Because the investigation is not finished yet.
  • Because warning the client (Tipping off) is a crime.

‚öñÔ∏è 6. Grammar: Passive Voice in Reporting

In legal and compliance reporting, we often use the Passive Voice to focus on the action or the funds, rather than the person doing it (who might be unknown).

Structure:
Object + be (was/were/has been) + Past Participle
Active: The criminal moved the funds.
Passive: The funds were moved (by the criminal).
Active: We must freeze the account.
Passive: The account must be frozen.
Active: Someone has laundered the money.
Passive: The money has been laundered.
