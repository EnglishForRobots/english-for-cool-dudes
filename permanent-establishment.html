üè¢ The Permanent Establishment concept

A Permanent Establishment, or PE, is a fixed place of business through which the business of an enterprise is wholly or partly carried on. The most common examples are a branch office, a factory, or a workshop. A PE triggers a tax liability in a foreign country for a multinational corporation.

The concept is generally defined in Article 5 of the OECD Model Tax Convention. The duration of activities is a key factor; for a construction site to be a PE, it typically must last for more than 12 months. However, under recent BEPS changes, activities such as providing services can create a PE after a shorter period, for example 6 months, depending on the tax treaty.
