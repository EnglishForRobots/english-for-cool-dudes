Can Artificial Intelligence replace Hollywood filmmakers? WSJ journalist Joanna Stern put this to the test by creating a short film using only AI tools like Google Veo and Runway. The result is a mix of impressive technology and hilarious bloopers.

While the AI could generate stunning visuals from a simple text prompt, the team faced a major challenge: keeping the characters consistent. In some shots, the protagonist looked like a video game character; in others, she defied the laws of gravity.

To fix this, they had to dissect the process, using reference images to guide the AI through multiple iterations. Even the voice acting was tricky‚ÄîAI tools struggled to capture the correct human inflection and sarcasm.

Ultimately, the experiment showed that while these tools can optimize workflows, human creativity is still the essential ingredient.

‚öôÔ∏è Passive Voice & Process

When describing how something was made‚Äîespecially in tech or science‚Äîwe often use the Passive Voice because the action is more important than the person doing it.

Active: Humans wrote the script.
Passive: The script was written by humans.
Active: We fed the image into the tool.
Passive: The image was fed into the tool.
Active: AI generated the audio.
Passive: The audio was generated by AI.
